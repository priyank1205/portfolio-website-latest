Mega Poker — App Flows Redesign

Redesigned wallet, tournament, and withdrawal flows and screens for the Android app of Mega Poker.

Client

Mega Poker

Timeline

2024 (2 months)

My Role

Product Designer, Design Lead

Team

Me (designer), 1 product manager, CEO

🦚 About the project

Mega Poker was a real-money gaming startup that gave players a platform to play poker on its mobile apps. (It shut its operations after online poker was banned in India.)

The CEO approached me with a problem: users were withdrawing a large percentage of the money from their in-app wallets, and the business wanted users to keep more of their money in the wallets and circulate it on the platform. They also wanted several flows and screens redesigned.

I worked closely with the product manager, and we provided weekly updates to the CEO. I was responsible for the complete end-to-end designs.

🏝️ The Flows and Screens

Self-Transfer

To increase cash flow within the system and reduce money leaving it, the product gently nudges users with bonus offers, encouraging them to transfer money from their winnings wallet to their deposit wallet.

Add Cash Flow

They wanted to incorporate offers and a relatively complex locked-bonus system into their cash deposit flows. The bonus was meant to compensate for the tax deducted at source (TDS) on deposits.

A note on visual style: Mega Poker was migrating from an old visual style (which was all over the place) to a new, locked style. For some flows they wanted the new style and for some the old one — so you might see discrepancies, but all of it was very much intentional.

Tournaments Redesign

The tournaments screen had a very outdated design and didn’t surface many important details, so they wanted it redesigned completely — without touching the other elements on the page, i.e. the header with the “add cash” chip.
