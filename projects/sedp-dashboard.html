Economic Dashboard Redesign — SEDP

Redesigned the flagship Socio-Economic Dashboard for the Center for Economic Data & Analysis.

Client

Ashoka University, India

Timeline

2023–2024

My Role

Product Designer, UX Designer, Design Lead, UI Designer

Team

Me (sole designer), 2 developers, 1 head of department

🦚 About the project

The Center for Economic Development (CEDA) at Ashoka University works on socio-economic data and facilitates informed discourse on key economic and social developments in India.

They wanted their flagship socio-economic dashboards redesigned for:

  1. Reducing complexity and making them more intuitive
  2. Making the visual language consistent across their various dashboards
  3. Making them visually attractive, clean, and modern
  4. Acquiring a new audience by facilitating the sharing of charts on social media

I was responsible for the complete end-to-end designs.

👹 The Problem

The existing dashboard was very complex to use, had bad UX and an outdated UI. The interface required a high learning curve and was therefore used only by a handful of academicians and researchers.

  1. The main function — visualized data — was below the fold for no reason
  2. The colors used for the visualization were arbitrary and didn’t follow the brand
  3. The language used (like “Binning”, “Quintile”) was very complex for regular users
  4. The timeline selector was completely detached from the visualization itself

🌳 The Redesign

Socio-Economic Data Portal (SEDP)

I designed the dashboard as a non-scroll, single-page application that showcases the visualization, legends, selectors, compare features, and intuitive navigation — all in one well-organized layout.

A new landing page

Since this was CEDA’s flagship dashboard, I also designed a landing page with:

  1. Featured charts (based on user data)
  2. Search bar for easy navigation
  3. Chart-browsing experience — charts categorised into socio-economic themes

Shareable image for social media

CEDA wanted their charts to reach a wider audience through a robust share feature. I designed a 1:1 template for all three chart types, ready to share on any social platform.

🏅 Outcome

CEDA does not track user data for their dashboards, so quantitative adoption and retention metrics weren’t available. But the larger faculty at CEDA and other departments at Ashoka University use the dashboards regularly for their own research, which brought a lot of qualitative feedback: I received appreciation and approval from the faculty at CEDA and Ashoka University.

I was also told they saw a larger audience than the previous base of researchers and academicians — visible purely through the engagement they were seeing on social media.

This was part of an overall redesign of three of their dashboards. The other two are documented in the Ecometer & Agrimarket case study.
