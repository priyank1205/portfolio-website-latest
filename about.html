How I work

  1. Design with empathy.
  2. Think like a product owner.
  3. Build with curiosity.

I chose interaction design because it sits at the intersection of psychology and technology, two lifelong curiosities. What excites me isn't making interfaces prettier: it's understanding how people think, what businesses need, and where technology is headed, then connecting those dots into products people genuinely enjoy using. Lately that means AI-native building: prototypes become products faster, and designers become builders.

Off hours I go deep on whatever catches my attention. The wider your understanding of the world, the better your instincts when designing for it.
